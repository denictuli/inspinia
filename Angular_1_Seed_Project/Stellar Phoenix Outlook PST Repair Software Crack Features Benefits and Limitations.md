Here are some tips to prevent PST file corruption:

- Exit Outlook properly before you shut down your system. If you force quit Outlook or turn off your system while Outlook is running, you may damage the PST file. Always close Outlook from the File menu and wait for it to exit completely before shutting down your system[^1^] [^2^].
- Do not operate on large volume of emails at once. If you try to move, copy, delete, or send a large number of emails at once, you may overload the PST file and cause corruption. You should perform these operations in batches and avoid interrupting them[^1^] [^2^].
- Do not store your PST file on a network drive or server. Storing your PST file on a network location can expose it to network errors, power failures, or unauthorized access that can corrupt the file. You should store your PST file on your local drive and back it up regularly[^1^] [^2^].
